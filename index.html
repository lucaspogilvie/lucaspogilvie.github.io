State Bill Outcome


Once a state bill is introduced by a legislature, it undergoes a comprehensive process before being enacted into law. It must successfully navigate through both chambers of the state congress and ultimately receive the governor's signature. This process can often be time-consuming, making it crucial for civic-minded individuals to have insights into the bill's potential outcome at the time of its introduction. In this project, I utilized Natural Language Processing, Over Sampling techniques, and a Random Forest Classifier to predict the fate of state bills based on their titles and the majority party in the House, Senate, and Governorship of the respective state. With this approach, the model was able to correctly identify correctly 75% of the bills that passed.

Heart Disease Classification


The objective of this study was to develop precise machine learning classification models capable of predicting heart disease and identifying individuals at risk. To accomplish this, our team employed the CDC's BRFSS 2021 dataset, which encompasses health and demographic information from over 400,000 individuals. By employing techniques such as resampling and class weighting to address data imbalance, along with employing various classification models, we achieved a balanced accuracy of 77% and a recall score of 82%. In other words, the Random Forest model accurately identified 82% of individuals with heart disease. This model holds the potential to support healthcare professionals in targeting preventive measures, enhancing patient outcomes, and alleviating the burden of heart disease on the healthcare system.

Writing Classification


Just as people have different accents and speak differently in various parts of the United States, the question arose whether there are distinct characteristics in written communication that vary between cities. This study aimed to explore the potential differences in writing between two cities, Seattle and New York, and investigate whether it is possible to predict the origin of individuals based on their writing. By analyzing 4,000 written samples from the Seattle and New York City subreddits, the study sought to identify which city the written sample came from using Natural Language Processing techniques. The best model was able predict the city of origin with 89% accuracy.

Stop Sign Efficacy


Upon my relocation to Spokane, WA, I found myself puzzled by the residential intersections there. To my surprise, these intersections lacked any form of control, such as stop lights or stop signs. This unconventional setup led me to believe that they must be more hazardous compared to intersections with proper traffic control measures. However, after engaging in online discussions specific to Spokane, I discovered that this topic was quite contentious. Motivated by the desire to provide a data-driven perspective, I decided to delve into the issue using statistical analysis. Employing econometric principles, 1,500 intersections from Denver and Spokane were compared. The findings indicated that, while holding other factors constant, uncontrolled intersections indeed experienced a higher frequency of car crashes, with a statistically significant level of 1%.
